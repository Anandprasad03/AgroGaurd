Challenges We Solve

Unpredictable Spoilage

Farmers lack tools to estimate how long crops will remain fresh, leading to unnecessary waste.

Inefficient Logistics

Delayed or poorly planned transportation causes crops to rot before reaching markets.

Unreliable Market Demand

Farmers sell without knowing future demand or price trends, resulting in distress sales.

Soil Degradation

Continuous cultivation of the same crops reduces nutrients and increases pest risk.

Poor Storage Decision-Making

Farmers choose storage methods without scientific guidance, leading to unsuitable conditions and higher spoilage.

Disconnected Planning

Crop selection, storage, and selling are planned separately, increasing risk and reducing overall efficiency.
